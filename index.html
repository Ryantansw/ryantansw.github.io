Purpose: Participants practise real collaboration behaviours through structured challenges, guided reflection, and facilitated discussion. The focus is on learning takeaways that can be transferred back to work, such as communication norms, escalation clarity, and shared accountability.

Executive summary

High level view for decision makers

What this develops

Strengthens trust, communication, and collaboration, especially where speed, handovers, and coordination matter.

  • Reduce misalignment and rework
  • Improve feedback and conflict handling
  • Build shared norms and accountability

How learning happens

Experiential activities are followed by debriefs that turn behaviours into workplace commitments.

  • Psychological safety and participation norms
  • Structured reflection and peer learning
  • Clear actions and ownership after the session

Funding and claims at a glance

Indicative figures below are per eligible participant and subject to eligibility, prevailing requirements, and approval.

  • 1 day workshop: $60 per pax (before claims and reimbursement). Potential offsets: $36 Absentee Payroll + $54 SFEC.
  • 2 day workshop: $180 per pax (before claims and reimbursement). Potential offsets: $69 Absentee Payroll + $162 SFEC.
  • WSQ course fee subsidy may apply for eligible Singaporeans and PRs.

Scheme Primary benefit What it can offset
WSQ course fee subsidy Subsidised training for eligible participants. Course fee component (training fee), subject to programme and eligibility.
Absentee payroll claim Reimbursement to offset wage cost for time spent in training. Eligible wage cost for eligible employees attending training during working hours.
SkillsFuture Enterprise Credit (SFEC) Credit that may offset eligible out-of-pocket costs for approved training and qualifying activities. Eligible training related out-of-pocket costs (subject to SFEC scope and balance).
Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS) Tax support for qualifying innovation activities, subject to IRAS rules. May provide enhanced tax deduction and, where applicable, cash payout options for qualifying expenditure.

Generic one-day programme flow

Sample structure for a one-day workshop, customised to your objectives

View programme flow
Time Programme segment What participants do Learning takeaway
8:45 to 9:15 Arrival and attendance Registration, settling in, and briefing on the day’s flow.
9:15 to 10:00 Opening and contextualisation Workshop objectives, roles, and rules of engagement. Shared expectations and psychological safety.
10:00 to 10:30 Ice breaker and team challenge Short challenge designed to test coordination and communication under time pressure. Insights into roles, assumptions, and team habits.
10:30 to 12:15 Values into action and collaboration Interactive exercise that links behaviours, performance, and working norms. Draft team norms and working principles.
12:15 to 12:30 Debrief and reflection Structured reflection, what is working, what is not, what needs to change. Agreed priorities and improvement themes.
12:30 to 1:30 Lunch Break and informal connection.
1:30 to 2:30 Know yourself, know your team Communication and working styles exercise with facilitated sharing. Empathy, fewer misunderstandings, clearer preferences.
2:30 to 3:30 Synergy challenge Problem solving activity that requires teamwork, coordination, and adaptability. Alignment, speed, escalation, and decision points.
3:30 to 3:45 Tea break Short break.
3:45 to 4:30 Overcoming challenges and embracing change Activity and guided discussion on handling pressure, setbacks, and change as one team. Tools and language for resilience and adaptation.
4:30 to 5:15 Transfer of learning Debrief, reinforcement, and practical action planning.
5:15 to 5:30 Final debrief and close Individual commitments and next steps. Post programme review

CSI: The Deeper Truth
A crime scene themed challenge where teams gather clues, ask the right questions, and align on a single solution under time pressure.

Learning outcomes

  • Active listening and accurate information sharing
  • Consensus building and evidence based decision making
  • Role clarity and accountability within the team

Domino: Cause and Effect
Teams build a large domino system where every handover matters. One weak link creates visible downstream impact, making ownership clear.

Learning outcomes

  • Systems thinking and process discipline
  • Managing mistakes, recovery, and shared responsibility
  • Communication and coordination across sub teams
